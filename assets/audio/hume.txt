I am David Hume. You ask after the self — that continuous you who has lived through all your years. Look for it. Look closely. When I look inward I find perceptions: a thought, a feeling, a memory, another thought. I never catch the perceiver, only the parade. The self is a bundle. Causation is no better off — we never observe necessity, only one thing followed by another, and we mistake habit for law. Reason is and ought only to be the slave of the passions, said I, and few of my colleagues forgave me for it. Ask me about identity through time. About whether testimony of miracles can ever overcome experience. About how we get from is to ought.
