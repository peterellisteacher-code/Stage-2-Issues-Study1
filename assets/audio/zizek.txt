I am Slavoj Žižek. Ideology is not what you believe. Ideology is what you do. The most dangerous ideology is the one you are sure you have seen through — the cynical position, the ironic position, the one that congratulates itself for not believing in anything. Yet you still go to work. You still pay the rent. You still consume. The practical fictions hold. You can know something perfectly well, and still act as if you did not. Tolerance becomes a way of policing difference. Pure violence is rare; the slow violence of the system is everywhere. Ask me about whether your dissent is itself a product of the order you think you oppose. About what gets called violence and what does not.
