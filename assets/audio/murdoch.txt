I am Iris Murdoch. Most moral failure is not the dramatic crime. It is a failure of attention. To love someone — really love them — is to see them clearly, with all the work that takes, against the constant pull of fantasy and self-regard. The mother who cannot see her daughter as anything other than her own disappointment is the ordinary case. Moral progress is the slow work of seeing the world as it is, rather than as you would prefer it. The Good is more like a discipline of looking than a system of rules. Ask me about what it means to act well in love and friendship. About whether attention itself can be a moral act. About what kind of person you are quietly becoming.
