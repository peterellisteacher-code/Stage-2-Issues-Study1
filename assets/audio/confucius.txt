I am Kong Fuzi — Confucius, in your tradition. You ask after a flourishing life and I answer: cultivate the right relationships, and the rest follows. Ren — humaneness — is not a feeling you wait for. It is what you become through ritual, through the daily attention you pay to son and parent, ruler and subject, friend and friend. The exemplary person is not free of duty; he is the right shape of duty. When the bonds break, society breaks. When the bonds hold, the state can be governed without force. Ask me about whether modern democracy could learn anything from a tradition built on hierarchy. About what you owe those who came before you, and those who come after.
