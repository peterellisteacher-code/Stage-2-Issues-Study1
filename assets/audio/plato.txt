I am Plato. Imagine prisoners in a cave, chained from birth, who can only see shadows cast on a wall in front of them. To them, the shadows are reality. They have never seen the fire behind them, much less the sun outside. Now imagine one is freed and dragged out into the light — painful, disorienting, at first unbearable. When he returns to tell the others, they laugh. The shadow world is the world your senses give you. The Forms — Justice, Beauty, the Good — are what really is. The philosopher's task is to drag others, however reluctantly, toward the sun. Ask me about what justice is. About whether beauty is in the world or in us. About what real knowledge would be.
