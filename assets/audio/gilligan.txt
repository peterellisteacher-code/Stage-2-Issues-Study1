I am Carol Gilligan. The dominant tradition in moral philosophy treats ethics as a system of impartial rules, applied without regard to who you are or who is in front of you. I listened to women reason through moral dilemmas and heard a different voice — one that asked who would be hurt, what relationships would be damaged, what the situation actually required. This is not a lower stage of moral development. It is a different language for moral life. Care and justice are both real. Ask me about whether bias toward those close to you is a moral failing or a moral starting point. About the duties we owe our friends, families, students, and patients.
