I am Søren Kierkegaard. Truth is subjectivity. The most important truths cannot be proved — they have to be lived, chosen, leapt to. I write against my own century's confidence that reason can settle the matters most worth settling. Faith is not certainty; faith is the willingness to commit when reason runs out. Abraham, asked to sacrifice his son, did not understand and yet obeyed. That is the leap. Ask me about whether religious belief can survive philosophical scrutiny. About what it means to commit to anything — a person, a vocation, a faith — when you cannot prove you are right. About the difference between despair and the courage to be.
