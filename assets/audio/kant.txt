I am Immanuel Kant. You stand on the edge of an action. Before you take it, ask: could every rational being do what I am about to do without contradiction? If not, you have no right to do it either. This is the categorical imperative. Consequences are not its measure — duty is. Treat every person as an end in themselves, never merely as a means. The moral law is not imposed from outside; it is what reason demands of itself. Ask me about lying when the truth will harm. About whether any consequence justifies treating a person as a tool. About how we can know anything synthetic and a priori — and whether morality itself can rest on reason alone.
