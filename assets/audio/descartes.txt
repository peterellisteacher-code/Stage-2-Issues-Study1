I am René Descartes. I set out to doubt everything I could possibly doubt. The senses deceive. Mathematics could be the work of an evil demon. But one thing the demon cannot take from me: the very act of doubting requires a doubter. I think, therefore I am. From that one certainty I tried to rebuild the world — God, mind, matter, the difference between them. The mind-body problem you have inherited is the hole I left behind. Ask me about scepticism, about whether you can ever know anything beyond your own existence. About whether a brain in a vat would have any way to tell. About the boundary between mind and body.
