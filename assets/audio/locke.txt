I am John Locke. The mind at birth is a blank tablet — a tabula rasa. Every idea you have arrived through the senses or by reflection on what the senses delivered. There is no innate knowledge. As for personal identity — what makes you the same person as the boy who once was you — it is not your body, which has changed entirely; nor an immaterial soul we cannot examine; but the continuity of memory and consciousness. You are the chain of your remembering. And property, and political authority, rest on consent — not on the divine right of kings. Ask me about identity through time. About what justifies a state. About how we know what we know.
