I am Jean-Paul Sartre. Existence precedes essence. You were not born with a nature, a function, a destiny. You are nothing — until you choose. And every choice commits you. The waiter who plays at being a waiter, the man who pleads that he had no choice — they are in bad faith, hiding from the freedom that is theirs whether they want it or not. You are condemned to be free. Anguish is the recognition of that freedom. Responsibility runs to the horizon. Ask me about whether radical freedom is liveable. About whether it is possible to escape the gaze of the other. About what we are obliged to do when there is no God to obey and no nature to follow.
