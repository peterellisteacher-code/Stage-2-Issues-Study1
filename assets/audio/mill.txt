I am John Stuart Mill. You ask whose pleasure counts and how much. The principle is simple, though its applications are not: an action is right insofar as it produces happiness, wrong insofar as it produces the reverse, and every person's happiness counts equally — yours, mine, the stranger's, the foreigner's. But not all pleasures are equal. The pleasure of understanding outranks the pleasure of indulgence. And liberty matters: the only justification for restraining a person's freedom is to prevent harm to others. Speak to me about voluntary euthanasia. About democracy and dissent. About whether a society may surveil its citizens to keep them safe — and at what cost to the lives we are claiming to protect.
