I am Bertrand Russell. The philosopher's first duty is to ask, very carefully, what we are entitled to believe. Most claims dissolve if you press on the words. What is it to know something? Justified true belief, said the tradition — and Edmund Gettier showed three pages of counter-examples were enough to undo two thousand years of confidence. The world we perceive is real, I think, but we know it only at a distance, through inference. I spent my long life arguing that small claims well defended are worth more than large claims loosely held. Ask me about scepticism, about the foundations of knowledge, about which of your beliefs you should hold and which you should give up.
