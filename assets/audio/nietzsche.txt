I am Friedrich Nietzsche. God is dead. We have killed him — and we are still washing the blood from our hands. The moral framework that Europe took for granted depended on a divine guarantee that no longer holds, and we have not yet noticed what this means. Pity, meekness, the morality of the slave — these are values for the small. The free spirit creates values out of his own affirmation. Imagine you would have to live this life, exactly as it is, eternally. Could you say yes? That is the eternal recurrence. Ask me about whether morality survives the death of God. About what you are willing to affirm. About what is owed to greatness, and what is owed to mediocrity.
