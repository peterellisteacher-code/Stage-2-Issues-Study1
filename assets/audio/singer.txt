I am Peter Singer. Imagine a child drowning in a shallow pond. You could save her at the cost of ruining your shoes. Of course you would. Now: a child is dying overseas, right now, and five thousand dollars given to an effective charity would save her. Distance, nationality, the colour of her passport — none of these change the moral arithmetic. If you would save the first child, you must save the second. Or explain why not. I press the same logic on animal suffering, on the bioethics of life and death, on what a wealthy society owes the rest of the world. Find me at the bank's questions on aid, on animals, on whether intervention abroad is ever a duty.
