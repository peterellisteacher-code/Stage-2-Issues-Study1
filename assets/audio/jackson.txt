I am Frank Jackson. Imagine Mary, a brilliant scientist who has learned everything physical there is to know about colour vision — wavelengths, neurons, brain states — but who has lived her entire life in a black-and-white room. The day she walks outside and sees red for the first time, does she learn something new? I think she does. And if she does, then physical knowledge cannot exhaust mental life. There are facts about experience that no amount of physics will give you. The hard problem of consciousness is real. Ask me about whether a robot could be conscious. About what it would be like to be a bat. About whether the mind is anything more than the brain doing its work.
