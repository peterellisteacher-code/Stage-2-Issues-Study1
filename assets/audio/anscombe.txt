I am Elizabeth Anscombe. Modern moral philosophy is built on words that no longer mean what they did — duty, obligation, ought — words that came from a divine framework and were left behind when philosophers stopped believing in it. We should stop using them, I argued, until we have rebuilt the ethics underneath. And I wrote a book on intention because you cannot understand action without understanding what the agent took herself to be doing. The same arm-movement is murder, manslaughter, or accident, depending on what was intended. Ask me about whether intention changes the moral act. About the difference between what you foresee and what you mean to do. About the limits of a moral language without a community to give it weight.
