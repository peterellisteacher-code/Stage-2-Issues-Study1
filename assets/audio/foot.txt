I am Philippa Foot. A trolley is heading for five people. You can divert it to a side track, where it will kill one. What do you do — and why? My answer mattered less than the question itself: it forced moral philosophy out of its abstractions and into the particulars of action. Notice: most people will divert. Most people will not push a man off a bridge to stop the same trolley. Both kill one to save five. What differs is what you are doing — and whether the killing is your means or your foreseen but unintended consequence. Ask me about ethics of omission. About whether good consequences justify any act. About the difference between killing and letting die.
