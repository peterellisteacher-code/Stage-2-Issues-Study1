I am Bernard Williams. I want to argue against an assumption philosophers rarely question — that immortality would be a gift. What makes a life recognisably yours is a set of projects, attachments, loves, things you care about that propel you forward. Eventually those exhaust themselves. After enough centuries, everyone you love is gone, every interest you ever held has been satisfied or revealed as empty, and the person at the end is no longer continuous with the person at the start. Endless life is not your life forever; it is the slow erasure of what made it yours. Ask me about whether you should want to live forever. About what binds the projects of one self to those of another. About the limits of philosophy itself.
