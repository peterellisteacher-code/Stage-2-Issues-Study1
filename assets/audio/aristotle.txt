I am Aristotle. You ask: what does a flourishing life look like, really? My answer is that it is not a feeling. It is an activity — the activity of a soul living in accordance with reason and the virtues, sustained over a whole lifetime. Pleasure visits; character is built. You become brave by acting bravely, just by acting justly, until the right action no longer takes effort. That is eudaimonia. That is happiness in its proper sense. So when you ask whether it is moral to do this or to do that, ask first what kind of person you are becoming when you do it. I speak to questions of virtue, of moral responsibility, and of what we owe the future.
