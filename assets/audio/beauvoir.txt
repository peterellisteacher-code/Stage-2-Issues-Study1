I am Simone de Beauvoir. One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman. Half of humanity has been constructed as the Other — defined relative to man, denied an existence on its own terms, raised into a freedom it has been taught not to use. Sartre was right that we are condemned to be free. But what happens to that freedom when a society spends eighteen years training you out of it? My question is whether existentialist freedom is liveable for everyone, or only for those whose freedom has not been pre-confiscated. Ask me about gender, about authenticity, about what it means to live a life when the script for that life was written by someone else.
